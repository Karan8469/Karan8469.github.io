The First Law of Thermodynamics

ŒîU = Q - W

This simple equation is the culmination of everything. It's the law of Energy Conservation: the change in a system's internal energy must be perfectly accounted for by the energy that crosses its boundary as heat or work.

Key Formulas for Numericals

Here are some essential formulas for quantifying energy and its transformations in thermodynamic problems.

Kinetic Energy

Energy due to motion.

$$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

Where: $m$ is mass, $v$ is velocity

Potential Energy

Energy due to position.

$$PE = mgz$$

Where: $m$ is mass, $g$ is gravitational acceleration, $z$ is elevation

First Law of Thermodynamics

Energy conservation for a closed system.

$$\Delta U = Q - W$$

Where: $\Delta U$ is change in internal energy, $Q$ is heat transfer, $W$ is work done

Boundary Work (Constant Pressure)

Work done by expansion/compression at constant pressure.

$$W = P\Delta V$$

Where: $P$ is pressure, $\Delta V$ is change in volume

Sensible Heat Transfer

Heat transfer causing temperature change without phase change.

$$Q = mc\Delta T$$

Where: $m$ is mass, $c$ is specific heat, $\Delta T$ is change in temperature

Latent Heat Transfer

Heat transfer causing phase change at constant temperature.

$$Q = mL$$

Where: $m$ is mass, $L$ is latent heat of fusion/vaporization

The Second Law: The Arrow of Time

While the First Law says energy is conserved, the Second Law tells us how it spreads. It introduces Entropy (S), a measure of disorder or randomness. The entropy of an isolated system always increases over time.

Low Entropy

Ordered State

‚Üí

High Entropy

This is why heat spontaneously flows from hot to cold, and why a shuffled deck of cards is unlikely to sort itself. The universe tends towards chaos.
